Welcome! We are a group of neuroscientists and mental health researchers (at Columbia University, University of Montreal and New York State Psychiatric Institute) who want to accelerate brain science by making neuroscience more accessible and engaging to everyday people. Before we embark on this endeavor, we want to know what you think

Have you ever had an MRI scan as part of clinical evaluation or while volunteering for a MRI study? If you did, then you can get a digital copy of your scan (usually on a CD). However, opening and accessing these files to view them is difficult. We are developing a tool that will help you upload your brain scan and get a beautiful, interactive, 3D visualization of your own brain. Click here for a work in progress demo, which is based on one of our team member's brain.

We want to apply this tool to crowdsource brain imaging data, boost sample sizes for mental health research and collect important information, such as traits related to sex and gender, that are not typically examined in brain imaging research. These include sexual orientation, gender role identity (i.e., femininity and masculinity), and gender identity. We want to better understand the relationship between sex and gender, the brain and risk and resilience for these conditions in order to help guide development of more effective mental health treatments.

We greatly appreciate your input, even if you have never had a brain MRI scan. In the future we aim to develop tools that will help you find a research study in your area that you can participate in to get a brain scan for free.
