MyBrainandMe.Org: An online resource for user-driven data sharing of structural MRI brain imaging and behavioral data.

Why is this study being done?

Thanks for your interest in MyBrainandMe.org. Before you upload your brain scan, we would like to inform you about what this research involves. We will also inform you of any potential risks and/or benefits of your participation.

MyBrainandMe.org is a website where you can learn more about neuroscience and your brain. You will also help scientists to make new discoveries about how the brain works. The site is not-for-profit and directed by Dr. Pantazatos in the Brain Imaging Lab in the Molecular Imaging and Neuropathology Division at the New York State Psychiatric Institute.

If you previously had a structural MRI brain scan for research or clinical purposes, you can upload it to MyBrainandMe.org. Your data will contribute to a growing neuroscience resource. This resource may be made available to scientific researchers around the world. You will also have an opportunity to learn neuroanatomy using your own brain. There are no direct benefits from participating in this study.

Do I have to be in the study?

Participation in this research study is voluntary. You are free to withdraw consent and stop participating at any time.

What will happen to me if I’m in the study?

You will complete a brief personal profile and upload a digital copy of your MRI. You will also be asked to complete brief surveys or tasks in your web browser. These surveys and tasks take about 30 minutes to complete in total. You can take a break in between each survey or task. Some tasks measure things like your reaction time in response to pictures. Other tasks ask you questions about your emotions, feelings, life experiences, and habits. We may contact you in the future to ask if you are interested in completing additional tasks.

After uploading your MRI scan, you will have the opportunity to view it in a web-browser. Our site will label each brain region so that you can learn brain anatomy on your own brain.

Will anything bad happen to me?

There are no physical health risks associated with this study. If we learn of any reasons why you might change your mind about being in the study, we will tell you.

The study requires you to upload data and provide information. There is some risk to your personal identifying information. We will to the best of our ability protect your data from any security breaches. However, we cannot guarantee against any breach. We believe that the security threat to your personal identifying information is low.

Can this study help me?

By participating in this study, you will learn more about neuroscience and neuroanatomy using your own brain. You will not learn anything about your brain specifically through participating in this study. Rather, you may learn about the names of different parts of the brain, displayed on your own brain scan. You will also contribute to our understanding of how the brain works. You will not benefit medically by participating in this study.

Will people know that I am in the study?

Your data will be kept in a secure database. It will be shared with other researchers. However, your privacy is protected because shared data will not include any identifying information about you.

This research is covered by a Certificate of Confidentiality from the Department of Health and Human Services. This means we will not share your information with anyone else without your permission. An exception is if there is a law that requires us to share your information. Some examples are laws that may require us to report child abuse, plans to harm yourself or others, or contagious diseases. However, this exception does not apply to any legal proceedings. This exception does not apply if you give your permission to share your data. This exception does not apply if your data is used for other scientific research. This is allowed by federal regulations protecting research subjects.

A Certificate of Confidentiality does not prevent you or a family member from sharing your data with others. If you give written permission, then we may share your data with others. We may share your data if it is requested by groups that are funding this research. These funders include people from state or federal agencies. Your data may also be shared if it is needed by the federal Food and Drug Administration (FDA). Your information will be available to research staff. Your data will also be available to people from state and regulatory institutions. These people may review your data as part of routine audits.

Will I get anything if I decide to be in the study?

You will not be paid for your participation in this study.

Is there anything else that I should know about the study?

See our FAQ if you have any questions. Dr. Spiro Pantazatos oversees the study. Dr. Jeffrey Miller assists with the study. The research team will try to answer any questions you have. If you have questions, you may contact us at 646-774-8710. You may also write an email to spiro.pantazatos@nyspi.columbia.edu.
